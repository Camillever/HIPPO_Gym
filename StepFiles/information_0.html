Information Section

Background Information:

Fingerprints can be classified into impressions (left) and latent fingerprints (right). Fingerprint impressions are acquired under controlled conditions and have high quality. Latent fingerprints are unintentionally left by someone when manipulating objects and have low quality.

Latent fingerprint identification is usually related to forensic applications and has success rates as low as 71.32%. Consequently, latent fingerprint identification is an area of constant evolution, aiming to find new methods that help obtain better identification rates. Minutiae are the basic features used to represent fingerprints.

Latent fingerprint identification:

Marking minutiae in a latent fingerprint then searching a background database for impressions that are most similar to this minutiae patterns. Minutiae are points where the fingerprint ridges become discontinuous. There are many types of minutiae, but the pertinent ones are: line endings (left) and bifurcations (right).

  • Line ending: when the ridges come to an end.
  • Bifurcation: when the ridge splits in two.
​

Study Structure:

You will be asked to mark a minimum of 7 minutiae per fingerprint for a total of 30 different fingerprints. After you submit the minutiae, you will be able to review your score as well as your performance in comparison to an expert.

Score change functionality:

You are given the ability to request feedback using the “Get Feedback” button. This will turn most of your minutiae either green or red, as well as show a number next to them. If a minutia is green, then removing it may decrease your score. If a minutia is red, then removing it may increase your score. The number beside your minutia indicates how confident our algorithm is of the potential score change on a scale of 0-3.
